Clinical Decision Support via Integrated Patient Behavior & Risk Pattern Mining
CISC 483: Data Mining · Python · scikit-learn · mlxtend · NetworkX · Apriori · K-Means · PageRank · Collaborative Filtering · Decision Trees

Modular clinical decision support framework integrating multiple interpretable data mining techniques on synthetic EHR data.

  • Built a multi-module clinical decision support pipeline using synthetic EHR data (Synthea) to model patient behavior, treatment patterns, and risk stratification.
  • Applied association rule mining (Apriori) to symptom–medication–outcome triplets, extracting interpretable rules and adapting support thresholds for dataset scale.
  • Engineered patient-level features (age, encounter count, medication count, systolic blood pressure) and performed K-Means clustering to identify patient subgroups.
  • Constructed a directed graph of patient care transitions and applied PageRank-based centrality to identify high-impact care states and transition pathways.
  • Implemented collaborative filtering (user–user and item–item) using cosine similarity to recommend medications based on peer patient histories.
  • Trained an interpretable decision tree classifier (entropy / ID3-style) to predict high-risk vs. low-risk patients, evaluating precision, recall, and accuracy.
  • Emphasized interpretability through visualizations (PCA cluster plots, rule confidence charts, PageRank graphs) and clinical narrative analysis.
